## Bookkeeping Cheat Sheet

### Double-Entry Rules

| Account Type | Increases | Decreases |
|-------------|-----------|-----------|
| Assets | Debit | Credit |
| Expenses | Debit | Credit |
| Drawings | Debit | Credit |
| Liabilities | Credit | Debit |
| Capital | Credit | Debit |
| Income/Revenue | Credit | Debit |

### VAT Rates (Ireland 2025-2026)

| Rate | Category |
|------|----------|
| 23% | Standard (most goods/services) |
| 13.5% | Reduced (construction, tourism, food, energy) |
| 9% | Second reduced (hotels, newspapers) |
| 0% | Zero-rated (exports, basic food, children's clothes) |
| Exempt | Finance, insurance, education, healthcare |

### VAT Calculations

```
Net × 23% = VAT
Net + VAT = Gross
Gross ÷ 1.23 = Net
Gross − Net = VAT
```

### Bookkeeping Cycle

```
Source Documents → Daybooks → Ledgers → Trial Balance → Financial Statements
```

### Key Formulas

| Item | Formula |
|------|---------|
| Cost of Sales | Opening Stock + Purchases − Closing Stock |
| Gross Profit | Sales − Cost of Sales |
| Net Profit | Gross Profit − Expenses |
| Accounting Equation | Assets = Liabilities + Capital |
| Closing Balance (TB) | Debtors Control = Σ Individual Debtors |
| VAT Payable | Output VAT − Input VAT |

### Bank Reconciliation Flow

```
Cash Book Balance
  + Unpresented Cheques
  − Unlodged Lodgements
  +/− Cash Book Adjustments (bank charges, direct debits)
= Bank Statement Balance
```

## Payroll Cheat Sheet

### Tax Bands (Single Person — Indicative 2025-2026)

| Band | Rate | Cut-Off |
|------|------|---------|
| Standard Rate | 20% | Up to ~€42,000 |
| Higher Rate | 40% | Above ~€42,000 |

### Tax Credits (Indicative 2025-2026)

| Credit | Single | Married |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Personal Tax Credit | ~€1,875 | ~€3,750 |
| Employee (PAYE) Credit | ~€1,875 | ~€1,875 each |
| Home Carer Credit | — | Up to ~€1,800 |
| Single Person Child Carer | ~€1,650 | — |

### USC Rates (Indicative)

| Income Band | Rate |
|-------------|------|
| Up to €12,012 | 0.5% |
| €12,013 − €25,760 | 2% |
| €25,761 − €70,044 | 4.5% |
| €70,045+ | 8% |

### PRSI (Class A — Most Employees)

| | Rate | Threshold |
|---|------|-----------|
| Employee PRSI | 4% | Earnings > €352/week |
| Employer PRSI | 11.05% | All earnings |
| PRSI exempt | 0% | Earnings ≤ €352/week |

### Net Pay Calculation

```
Gross Pay
  − PAYE (Income Tax)
  − PRSI (Employee share)
  − USC
  − Pension (if applicable)
  − Other deductions (union dues, health insurance, court orders)
= Net Pay (Take-Home Pay)
```

### Employer PRSI Rates (Class A)

| Weekly Earnings | Employer Rate |
|----------------|--------------|
| Up to €441 (low pay exemption) | 8.5% |
| €441.01+ | 11.05% |

### Cumulative Payroll Formula

```
Each Pay Period:
  Cumulative Gross Pay to Date × Tax Rate = Cumulative Tax
  Less: Cumulative Tax Credits
  Less: Tax Paid in Previous Periods
= Tax Due This Period
```

### Key Revenue Deadlines

| Return | Frequency | Due Date |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| P30 | Monthly | 23rd of following month |

| P60 | Annual | Give to employee by mid-February |

### Emergency vs Cumulative

| | Emergency (Week 1) | Cumulative |
|---|-------------------|------------|
| Tax Rate | 40% | 20% / 40% |
| Tax Credits | None applied | Applied proportionally |
| Calculation | Each week in isolation | Year-to-date cumulatively |
| Refund | No | Automatic catch-up |
| When used | No RPN available | RPN available |

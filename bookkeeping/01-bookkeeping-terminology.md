# Learning Outcome 1 — Bookkeeping Terminology

**Objective:** Explain the key terminology associated with the recording and maintenance of bookkeeping records, using manual and computerised systems.

---

## Core Concepts

### The Accounting Equation

```
Assets = Liabilities + Capital (Owner's Equity)
```

- **Assets** — what the business owns (cash, equipment, inventory, debtors)
- **Liabilities** — what the business owes (creditors, loans, VAT owed)
- **Capital** — owner's investment + retained profits

### Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Ledger** | Main set of accounts where transactions are posted |
| **Daybook** | Book of first entry where transactions are initially recorded |
| **Trial Balance** | List of all ledger balances to check debits = credits |
| **Debtor** | Person who owes money to the business |
| **Creditor** | Person the business owes money to |
| **VAT** | Value Added Tax — a consumption tax on goods/services |
| **Bank Reconciliation** | Process of matching bank statement to cash book |
| **Petty Cash** | Small cash fund for minor expenses |
| **Double Entry** | Every transaction has a debit and credit of equal value |
| **Depreciation** | Allocation of asset cost over its useful life |

### Double-Entry Bookkeeping Rules

| Account Type | Increases with | Decreases with |
|-------------|---------------|---------------|
| Assets | Debit | Credit |
| Expenses | Debit | Credit |
| Drawings | Debit | Credit |
| Liabilities | Credit | Debit |
| Capital | Credit | Debit |
| Income | Credit | Debit |

### Source Documents

- **Invoice** — request for payment from seller to buyer
- **Credit Note** — reduction of amount owed (returns/refunds)
- **Receipt** — proof of payment received
- **Bank Statement** — record of bank transactions
- **Petty Cash Voucher** — record of small cash payment
- **Cheque** — written order to pay a specified amount

### Manual vs Computerised Systems

**Manual**: Physical books (daybooks, ledgers), handwritten entries, manual calculations, physical filing.

**Computerised**: Accounting software (Sage/QuickBooks), automated posting, instant calculations, digital backup.

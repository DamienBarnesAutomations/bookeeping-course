# Learning Outcome 5 — Changes in Personal Tax

**Objective:** Demonstrate the changes in personal tax due to various factors including mid-year commencement and leaving of employment, changes in credits, and refunds.

---

## Mid-Year Commencement of Employment

### Scenario
Employee Mary starts work in **Week 20** of the tax year with a new employer.

### Cumulative Effect
Under the cumulative system, Mary's tax credits and cut-off point are still full-year amounts allocated proportionally to the remaining weeks:

- Annual tax credit: €3,750
- Remaining weeks: 32 (weeks 20-52)
- Weekly credit for remaining weeks: €3,750 / 52 × 52 = €72.12 per week (full rate still)

Wait — no. Under cumulative, the credits are allocated evenly across 52 weeks. So each week gets €72.12 regardless of when the employee starts. But the cumulative calculation means:

### Example: Mary Starting Week 20

**Week 20 Payroll:**
| | Amount |
|---|--------|
| Gross Pay | €800 |
| Cumulative Gross (Week 20 only) | €800 |
| Tax @ 20% | €160.00 |
| Cumulative Tax Credits (1 week × €72.12) | (€72.12) |
| **PAYE Due** | **€87.88** |

Mary gets her full weekly tax credit in week 20. No credit is lost for the weeks she wasn't employed.

## Mid-Year Leaving of Employment

### Employee Leaves in Week 30

The employer issues a **P45** showing:
- Gross pay to date: €24,000
- Tax paid to date: €2,636.40
- Tax credits used to date: €2,163.60 (30 weeks × €72.12)
- Week 1 / Month 1 or Cumulative basis

### P45 Details (Used by Next Employer)
| Box | Value |
|-----|-------|
| Gross Pay to Date | €24,000 |
| Tax Deducted to Date | €2,636.40 |
| Tax Credits Used (if Week 1) | — |
| Basis | Cumulative |

The next employer uses the P45 figures to continue the cumulative calculation.

## Changes in Tax Credits

### Factors That Change Tax Credits

| Change | Effect on Credits |
|--------|------------------|
| Marriage/Civil Partnership | Can transfer credits between spouses |
| Birth of child | May increase credits |
| Health expenses | Claim tax relief at year-end |
| Tuition fees | Third-level fee relief |
| Remote working | Claim €3.20/day working from home |
| Change in job | Credits re-allocated by Revenue |

### Mid-Year Credit Change Example

Employee had €3,750 annual credits. In **Week 16**, Revenue issues new RPN with increased credits of €4,000 (due to health expenses relief).

**Before change (Weeks 1-15):**
- Weekly credit: €72.12
- Total credits used: 15 × €72.12 = €1,081.80

**After change (Week 16 onwards):**
- New weekly credit: €4,000 / 52 = €76.92
- Cumulative credits available by Week 16: 16 × €76.92 = €1,230.72
- Already used: €1,081.80
- Remaining credits in Week 16: €148.92

**Week 16 PAYE:**
| | Amount |
|---|--------|
| Gross Pay | €800 |
| Cumulative Gross | €12,800 |
| Tax @ 20% | €2,560.00 |
| Cumulative Credits Available | (€1,230.72) |
| Cumulative PAYE | €1,329.28 |
| LESS PAYE already paid (Weeks 1-15) | (€1,081.80) |
| **PAYE Week 16** | **€247.48** |

## Refunds

### When Refunds Happen
1. Overpaid emergency tax — refunded automatically under cumulative
2. Increased tax credits — cumulative catch-up refunds excess tax
3. Leaving employment mid-year with overpayment
4. Excessive deductions due to wrong tax band

### Refund Process
- Under cumulative system, refund happens **automatically** through payroll
- Software calculates lower (or negative) tax deduction in the current period
- Employee receives more net pay in that period

### Example: Refund Due to Credit Increase (Continuing from Above)

Before credit change, employee paid **€72.12 per week** in PAYE.
After credit change, cumulative catch-up means the software gives **€148.92 in credits for Week 16** instead of €76.92.

The difference (€72.00) effectively refunds the extra tax paid in the first 15 weeks.

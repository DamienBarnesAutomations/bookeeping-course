# Learning Outcome 8 — Married Couple Assessment

**Objective:** Assess the effect of using alternative assessment methods to calculate the annual tax liability of married couples.

---

## Assessment Methods for Married Couples

In Ireland, married couples (and civil partners) can choose from three assessment methods:

| Method | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Joint Assessment** | Default — one spouse is assessable, credits/cut-off can be transferred |
| **Separate Assessment** | Each spouse assessed independently on their own income |
| **Separate Treatment** | Each spouse treated as single for tax purposes |

---

## 1. Joint Assessment (Default)

One spouse is the **assessable spouse** (usually the higher earner). Tax credits and cut-off points can be transferred between spouses.

### Tax Credits Available

| Credit | Joint Amount |
|--------|-------------|
| Married Personal Tax Credit | €3,750 (vs €1,875 single) |
| Employee PAYE Credit | €1,875 (each spouse) |
| Home Carer Credit | Up to €1,800 |

### Standard Rate Cut-Off

| Status | Cut-Off |
|--------|---------|
| Single Person | €42,000 |
| Married (1 income) | €51,000 |
| Married (2 incomes) | €51,000 + up to €33,000 transferable band |

### Example: Joint Assessment, Two Incomes

**John:** €50,000 gross
**Mary:** €30,000 gross

```
John's Tax:
  First €42,000 @ 20%              = €8,400
  Remaining €8,000 @ 40%           = €3,200
  Total Gross Tax                   = €11,600
  Less: Married Credit              (€3,750)
  Less: PAYE Credit (John)          (€1,875)
  Less: PAYE Credit (Mary)          (€1,875)
  Less: Home Carer (if applicable)  (€0)
  John's Total Tax                  = €4,100

  (Mary's income is covered within 
   the standard rate band because 
   €50,000 + €30,000 = €80,000,
   but the band is up to ~€84,000
   with transferable band)
```

Actually, let me calculate this more carefully.

### Detailed Example: Joint Assessment

**Spouse 1 (Assessable):** €55,000
**Spouse 2:** €25,000

**Total Income:** €80,000

**Standard Rate Cut-Off:** €51,000 (base for married) + €33,000 (transferable band) = €84,000

Since total income (€80,000) is below €84,000, **all income is taxed at 20%**.

| | Amount |
|---|--------|
| Tax @ 20% on €80,000 | €16,000 |
| Less: Married Personal Credit | (€3,750) |
| Less: PAYE Credit (Spouse 1) | (€1,875) |
| Less: PAYE Credit (Spouse 2) | (€1,875) |
| **Total Tax Liability** | **€8,500** |

---

## 2. Separate Assessment

Each spouse is treated independently for:

- Own income
- Own tax credits
- Own standard rate cut-off

**No transfer of credits or bands.**

### Example: Separate Assessment

**Spouse 1:** €55,000
**Spouse 2:** €25,000

**Spouse 1:**
| | Amount |
|---|--------|
| First €42,000 @ 20% | €8,400 |
| Next €13,000 @ 40% | €5,200 |
| Gross Tax | €13,600 |
| Less: Personal Credit | (€1,875) |
| Less: PAYE Credit | (€1,875) |
| **Tax Due** | **€9,850** |

**Spouse 2:**
| | Amount |
|---|--------|
| First €25,000 @ 20% | €5,000 |
| Less: Personal Credit | (€1,875) |
| Less: PAYE Credit | (€1,875) |
| **Tax Due** | **€1,250** |

**Total Tax (Separate): €9,850 + €1,250 = €11,100**

---

## 3. Separate Treatment

Each spouse is treated as **single** for all tax purposes.

- Same as separate assessment in calculation
- Cannot claim married credits or band
- Result is same as two single people

**Total Tax: Usually the highest of the three options.**

---

## Comparison Table

| Method | Spouse 1 Tax | Spouse 2 Tax | Total |
|--------|-------------|-------------|-------|
| Joint Assessment | €8,500 | €0 (included) | **€8,500** |
| Separate Assessment | €9,850 | €1,250 | **€11,100** |
| Separate Treatment | €9,850 | €1,250 | **€11,100** |

**Best option: Joint Assessment — saves €2,600 in this scenario.**

## Home Carer Tax Credit

Available to married couples where one spouse cares for a dependent person (child, elderly relative).

| Income of Carer | Credit |
|----------------|--------|
| Up to €7,200 | Full credit (~€1,800) |
| Between €7,200 - €10,200 | Reduced credit |
| Over €10,200 | No credit |

---

## Effect of Marriage Mid-Year

When a couple marries during the tax year:

- They can claim **full year** married credits and bands
- Any tax already paid at single rate is adjusted
- Can backdate to date of marriage or start of tax year

### Example: Married in October 2026 (Week 40)

**Before Marriage:** Both on single credits (€1,875 each)
**After Marriage:** Switch to married assessment, claim unused credits from start of year.

Revenue will issue a new RPN with married credits. Cumulative system automatically refunds any overpaid tax.

---

## Choosing the Best Method

| Situation | Recommended |
|-----------|------------|
| One earner significantly higher | Joint Assessment |
| Both earners similar income | Joint or Separate (calculate both) |
| One spouse has tax losses/reliefs | Joint Assessment |
| One spouse has significant deductions | Joint Assessment |
| Privacy/separation | Separate Assessment |
